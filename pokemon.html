POKÉMON TEAMS

My Introduction to Pokemon

In terms of videogames, Pokemon has played a large role in my personal relationship with videogames. It is one of the first games I ever remember sinking countless hours into to grind. A little backstory to where my pokemon journey started. As a kid I had a wii, and on that wii I had a game called Pokemon Battle Revolutions. This was the pokemon 3-D battle counterpart to the generation 4 games (primarily diamond pearl and platinum). I loved this game to death but had never played a real pokemon game before, so upon learning that I could put custom pokemon into the game if I had one of the pokemon games on the DS, I practically forced my dad to buy me a copy of Pokemon Platinum, which became my very first pokemon game. I instantly fell in love with the game and I remember having so much fun from the moment I started playing all the way through beating the elite four and Sylvia. After beating the game, I uploaded my pokemon to battle revolutions and was instanyl captivated by seeing the team I had grown so attached to battle opponents in 3-D. I eventually saught to put as many pokemon into the game as I could, so I started catching more pokemon in platinum, giving them an xp-share, and grinding the elite 4 until they evolved or got the movesI wanted them to have. This was an incredibly expansive task, especially once I found out about pal park. The quest to get more pokemon from other regions in my battle revolutions save mptovated me to expand my horizons beyond platinum. My first game post-platinum was heart gold, lent to me by a friend. I really enjoyed this game too, but I truly fell in love with the following game, pokemon emerald. I ordered this game and fire red around the same time and loved both games to death. Once I finished the game, I'd throw my pokemon in pal park, claim them on my platinum save, and repeat the process of grinding them up to have their desired movesets. Disaster struck one day in high school when I put my platinum cartridge into my original DSi. Upon opening the game, I was greeted by the blue screen of death and despair. The entire save had corrupted. All of the countless hours I sank into collecting and training probably around a couple hundred pokemon was gone in an instant. I was devistated. I genuinely think I cried (this was in my freshman year of highschool by the way). Luckily after forwarding the news to my friend Ben, he introduced me to the wonderful world of modding, and helped intorduce me to PKhex and let me use his modded 3ds and his copy of platinum to get not only my pokemon back, but any pokemon I wanted. This was huge and I was extremely grateful to have an outlet to complete the quest I started so long ago. I ended up having a copy of every single final stage of a pokemon from generations 1-4 and thier shiny variant and me and my frineds still play this game on my wii to this day.
